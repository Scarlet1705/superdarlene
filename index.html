DID YOU KNOW?

According to the World Bank, in 2020, 89.7% of Indonesia's population had access to basic water supply services, while 60.4% had access to safely managed water supply services. However, there are still disparities between urban and rural areas, with rural areas having lower access to basic and safely managed water supply services.

SOLAR RADIATION

Indonesia has a high solar radiation potential, with an average daily solar radiation of 4-6 kWh/m2 in most areas. According to the International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA), Indonesia's solar power potential is estimated to be around 207.7 GW, with the potential to generate around 280 TWh of electricity per year.
